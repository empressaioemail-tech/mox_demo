# Differentiation (the three-point thread)

> The "what makes this different" thread, woven into the surfaces — not bolted on
> at the end. Each point below has the demo-copy version (what is said), the surface
> it lands on, and the honesty constraint that keeps it true. WS-7 owns this thread;
> the surfaces consume it.

There are three points. They build: the unit of value (the atom), the engine of
value (two flywheels), and the proof you cannot argue with (the ground truth on
screen).

---

## 1. The treatment, not the data

**Copy.** "A dashboard shows you a number. We show you an atom. Every value on this
surface carries its own provenance, its reasoning, its confidence, and a
calibration state that tightens with use. You can see why the number is what it is,
where it came from, how sure the system is, and — once your data is wired — it gets
less wrong every month. The data is table stakes. The treatment of the data is the
product."

**Where it lands.** Everywhere a number appears: every value carries a
`ConfidenceChip` (state-visible) with a provenance drill behind it. The contrast is
sharpest on the Yardi layer (a number on a screen they already know) and the
investor room (where a static PDF becomes a drillable artifact).

**Honesty constraint.** The chip shows the real state. In this demo that state is
`baseline` / `provenance-backed` — backed by source and reasoning, not yet
calibrated on the prospect's outcomes. The "less wrong every month" claim is stated
as what the live earning loop produces over time, never as something already true
of a representative number (guardrails 1 and 2).

---

## 2. Two flywheels

**Copy.** "Two things compound here, and they compound separately. The first is
your private operating intelligence: your corrections, your outcomes, your history.
That never pools with anyone — it compounds on your own book and becomes a moat a
rival cannot copy, because they do not have your fifteen years of decisions. The
second is the shared ground truth — the code, the zoning, the parcel facts — and
that gets sharper because the whole network feeds it. You get a private flywheel
nobody can see and a shared flywheel everybody pays into. Most vendors give you
neither; the ones that pool your data give you the wrong one."

**Where it lands.** The flywheel context surface, and narratively at the
deposit-loop moment in the command surface (the private flywheel turning) and at the
plan-review / ground-truth layer in the spatial twin and investor room (the shared
flywheel paying off).

**Honesty constraint.** The deposit-loop moment shows the earning loop is *live*,
not that the displayed numbers are already calibrated (guardrail 2). The private
flywheel is described as compounding on *your* outcomes once wired — the demo shows
the mechanism, not a year of accrued calibration.

---

## 3. The ground truth is already built, and nobody else has it

**Copy.** "Here is the part you cannot fake and cannot buy off a shelf. We did not
hand-enter this. Our substrate produced the parcel, the zoning, and the code for
this site — with citations — and then it caught something: this is a five-story
building on MF-3 land, which caps at forty feet and thirty-six units an acre. It
needs a rezoning to MF-4 or a variance, and the system flagged it before a single
sheet was submitted. That is jurisdictional ground truth, already built, on the
real parcel. No other GP can put that on the table, and no general-purpose AI has
it. The plan review is the proof, on screen."

**Where it lands.** The spatial twin (the ground-truth layer above the unit atoms)
and the investor room (the plan-review findings section). This is the hero finding.

**Honesty constraint.** The plan-review atom carries the code citation, the as-of
date, and a `baseline` / `provenance-backed` confidence chip. The headline
(5 stories exceeds MF-3's 40 ft cap) is verified; the exact MF-4 height and the
variance path are verified through the substrate before the finding ships. We
surface the diligence; the GP makes the representations. Nothing here certifies the
deal.

---

## How the three connect (one sentence for the presenter)

The atom is the unit (point 1), two flywheels are how it compounds (point 2), and
the ground truth already in hand is the proof the flywheel has already turned where
it matters most (point 3) — which is exactly what de-risks the deal in the investor
room.
