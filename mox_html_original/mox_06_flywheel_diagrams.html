Mox intelligence

The flywheel

Every surface your team touches both uses the intelligence and feeds it. The more it is used, the sharper it gets, and you own all of it. Three views: how it turns, what stays private, and why it compounds against competitors.
1

The mechanic

consume, core to surfacecontribute, surface to core
The center is the data core with the gate on it. The surfaces around it are where the team works. A surface pulls calibrated context out of the core, the person acts, and the decision flows back in. The contribution does not just accumulate, it calibrates, so the core gets less wrong every month and every surface gets sharper.
2

What stays private, what is shared

yours, never pooledshared with the network
The gate routes deposits two ways. Operating decisions, how you run, underwrite, and close, go into your private intelligence and never get pooled. Only the non competitive layer, code and regulatory interpretation, flows into shared ground truth that gets sharper because other contractors and cities feed it too. Drawing that line sharply is what earns the trust of an operator whose data is their moat.
3

Why it compounds against competitors

The three arms feed each other. Manage produces operating reality that makes Invest underwrite better than the broker. Invest funds deals that flow to Build. Build's completed work flows back as sharper operating and underwriting inputs. Competitors run the same off the shelf tools and generic AI, none of which learn from their results. Mox compounds on its own outcomes, a gap rivals cannot close because they do not have Mox's history. Early the edge is modest, with time and use it gets dramatic.
